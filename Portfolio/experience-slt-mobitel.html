Program Overview

Completed a comprehensive 12-week industrial training program at Sri Lanka Telecom (SLT), working at the Batticaloa Operations and Maintenance Center (OPMC). This facility serves as a critical hub for telecommunications infrastructure management, network operations, and customer service delivery in the Eastern Province.

The internship provided hands-on experience across multiple operational departments, bridging theoretical knowledge with practical applications in modern telecommunications infrastructure. Working alongside experienced engineers and technicians, I gained deep insights into how large-scale telecommunications networks operate, maintain service quality, and evolve with new technologies.

Training Facility & Departments

  • Outside Plant Maintenance Center (OPMC): Infrastructure monitoring, network maintenance, and proactive service disruption prevention covering geographical service areas.
  • Service Assurance (SA) Department: Quality assurance, network performance monitoring, and customer service quality metrics.
  • Cable and Distribution Maintenance (CDM): Underground and overhead cable infrastructure, distribution point management, and fault rectification.
  • Main Distribution Frame (MDF): Central switching hub connecting exchange lines to customer premises with precision cable management.

ADSL System Architecture

ADSL (Asymmetric Digital Subscriber Line) technology enables high-speed internet delivery over existing copper telephone lines. The system architecture involves multiple interconnected components working seamlessly to provide reliable connectivity.

Component Function Technical Parameter
Splitter Separates voice and data signals on copper line High-pass filter (data) / Low-pass filter (voice)
ADSL Modem Modulates/demodulates digital signals VPI: 8, VCI: 35-36
DSLAM Concentrates ADSL lines at exchange ATM-based aggregation
Copper Line Medium for signal transmission Typical loop: 0-5.5 km distance
Router IP packet routing and NAT Bridge mode or routed mode

Copper vs. Fiber Technology Comparison

Characteristic Copper (ADSL) Fiber (FTTH)
Maximum Bandwidth ~24 Mbps 1 Gbps+
Distance Limitation 5.5 km maximum 10+ km possible
Signal Degradation Susceptible to interference Immune to EMI
Installation Cost Lower Higher initial cost
Future-Proofing Limited upgrade potential Scalable for future needs
Convergence Services Voice + Data Voice + Data + Video + IoT

Electronic Engineering Intern

Sri Lanka Railways, Rathmalana

A comprehensive 12-week industrial training program focused on railway electrical, electronic, and telecommunication systems across three critical engineering hubs.

October 2023 – January 2024
Railway Systems
Electronics & PCB
Hands-on Training

Program Overview

Completed a comprehensive 12-week industrial training program at Sri Lanka Railways, one of the nation's largest railway operators. The internship involved rotations through three critical engineering hubs, providing hands-on experience in diverse areas of railway electrical and electronic systems.

This intensive training program provided practical experience in real-world industrial environments, working alongside experienced engineers and technicians. The rotation model allowed exposure to different specialized areas, building a comprehensive understanding of modern railway infrastructure and maintenance practices.

Training Locations

  • Chief Mechanical Engineer (CME) Workshop, Rathmalana: Central hub for locomotive and rolling stock maintenance, featuring advanced diagnostic and repair equipment.
  • L.T.L Section, Maradana Railway Station: Signaling and track control operations, responsible for train detection and safety systems.
  • Signal & Telecommunication Workshop, Dematagoda: Communication infrastructure, signaling systems, and signal relay maintenance facility.

Technical Experience & Key Areas

Power Systems & Battery Management

Managed repair and maintenance of Lead Acid and Alkaline batteries critical for locomotive engine starting and compartment power systems.

  • Discharging tests on 200Ah Lead Acid batteries using specialized machinery
  • Voltage drop monitoring over 6-hour discharge cycles
  • Identification and replacement of damaged cells
  • Battery capacity assessment and troubleshooting
  • Safety protocols for hazardous battery handling
Image: Railway Battery Charging Station

HVAC & Air Conditioning Systems

Specialized in repair and maintenance of both Inverter and Non-inverter AC units in passenger compartments.

  • Evaporator and compressor unit repair and maintenance
  • Troubleshooting room and tube temperature sensors
  • PCB diagnostics and faulty component replacement
  • Refrigerant handling and system pressure testing
  • Climate control optimization for passenger comfort
Image: AC Compressor Unit

Electrical Machines & Motor Repair

Maintained and repaired DC brushed and brushless motor fans used throughout train compartments.

  • Armature and stator rewinding using manual coil winding machines
  • Bearing replacement and lubrication procedures
  • Brush inspection and commutator maintenance
  • Motor performance testing and load validation
  • Efficiency optimization and longevity improvements
Image: Manual Coil Winding Machine

Locomotive Electronics (Workshop 35)

Specialized in Electronic Card Section, performing precision repairs on control cards for M4 and M7 locomotive series.

  • PCB-level diagnostics and component identification
  • Precision soldering and desoldering techniques
  • Temperature monitor card restoration
  • Load regulator circuit repair and testing
  • Non-working component replacement and validation
Image: Technician on Locomotive PCB

Signaling & Track Control Systems

Maintained track circuits and point machines critical for railway safety and train detection systems.

  • Track Circuit maintenance using DC currents and relays
  • Train presence detection system operation
  • Point Machine DC series motor repair
  • Gear system maintenance and lubrication
  • Remote track switching control verification
Image: Track Point Machine

Telecommunications & Audio Systems

Repaired communication infrastructure essential for station and on-train announcements.

  • Dynamic microphone repair and maintenance
  • Amplifier circuit diagnostics and repair
  • Speaker system testing and replacement
  • Cat 6 cabling installation and troubleshooting
  • Relay-based signaling system maintenance
Image: Industrial Relay System
